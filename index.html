  <h3 style="font-family:var(--serif);font-size:24px;margin-bottom:12px;" class="fade-in">How prompting works in Krea</h3>

  <p class="prompt-intro fade-in">A prompt is the text instruction you give Krea to describe what the finished image should look like. In the Enhancer tool, your prompt works alongside your uploaded image: the AI uses both to generate the result. The image defines the geometry; the prompt defines everything else: materials, lighting, context, and mood.</p>


  <!-- The Prompt Formula -->

  <div class="prompt-formula fade-in">

    <h3>The Prompt Formula</h3>

    <p style="font-size:14px;color:rgba(255,255,255,0.6);margin-bottom:20px;position:relative;z-index:1;">Every strong architectural prompt follows the same basic structure:</p>

    <div class="formula-parts">

      <div class="formula-part">Building Type</div>

      <div class="formula-plus">+</div>

      <div class="formula-part">Materials</div>

      <div class="formula-plus">+</div>

      <div class="formula-part">Context</div>

      <div class="formula-plus">+</div>

      <div class="formula-part">Lighting</div>

      <div class="formula-plus">+</div>

      <div class="formula-part">Quality Keywords</div>

    </div>

    <div class="formula-example">Modern residential house, white stucco facade, large glass windows, concrete base, surrounded by mature trees and green lawn, warm late afternoon sunlight, photorealistic, architectural photography, 4K</div>

  </div>


  <!-- The Four Prompt Components -->

  <h3 style="font-family:var(--serif);font-size:24px;margin-bottom:20px;" class="fade-in">The Four Prompt Components</h3>


  <div class="prompt-components fade-in">

    <div class="prompt-comp">

      <div class="comp-num">1.</div>

      <h4>Materials</h4>

      <p class="comp-desc">Describe the primary surfaces of the building. Be specific — the more precise the material description, the more accurate the result.</p>

      <div class="prompt-level"><span class="level-label l1">Vague</span><span class="level-text">white walls</span></div>

      <div class="prompt-level"><span class="level-label l2">Better</span><span class="level-text">white smooth stucco facade, floor-to-ceiling glass, brushed concrete base</span></div>

      <div class="prompt-level"><span class="level-label l3">Best</span><span class="level-text">white smooth stucco, dark anodised aluminium window frames, board-formed concrete plinth</span></div>

    </div>

    <div class="prompt-comp">

      <div class="comp-num">2.</div>

      <h4>Context</h4>

      <p class="comp-desc">Describe the surrounding environment. This includes landscape, neighbouring buildings, and site character.</p>

      <ul class="comp-list">

        <li><strong>Residential</strong> — manicured lawn, mature oak trees, suburban street</li>

        <li><strong>Urban</strong> — city street, adjacent brick buildings, parked cars, pedestrians</li>

        <li><strong>Landscape</strong> — open countryside, rolling hills, gravel driveway</li>

        <li><strong>Waterfront</strong> — waterfront site, timber dock, calm water reflections</li>

      </ul>

    </div>

    <div class="prompt-comp">

    <h2 class="section-title">Tips & common mistakes</h2>

  </div>


  <div class="tips-grid fade-in">

    <div class="tips-col do">

      <h3><span class="icon-circle">✓</span> Tips for Better Results</h3>

      <div class="tip-item">Be specific with materials — the more precise, the better the AI interprets your intent</div>

      <div class="tip-item">Always include a lighting descriptor — omitting it produces inconsistent, flat results</div>

      <div class="tip-item">End every prompt with quality keywords — photorealistic, architectural photography, 4K</div>

      <div class="tip-item">Start mid-range on the creativity slider — adjust up or down based on your first result</div>

      <div class="tip-item">Use transparent background PNGs from Rhino — gives Krea full control over sky and context</div>

      <div class="tip-item">Generate 3–4 variations — small prompt changes can produce significantly different results</div>

    </div>

    <div class="tips-col dont">

      <h3><span class="icon-circle">✗</span> Common Mistakes to Avoid</h3>

      <div class="tip-item"><strong>Too vague</strong> — white building, nice light → use specific material and lighting terms</div>

      <div class="tip-item"><strong>No context</strong> — omitting surroundings produces floating, ungrounded renders</div>

      <div class="tip-item"><strong>No quality keywords</strong> — always end with photorealistic, 4K, architectural photography</div>

      <div class="tip-item"><strong>Overcrowding the prompt</strong> — keep prompts focused — 20–40 words is usually enough</div>

      <div class="tip-item"><strong>Ignoring the creativity slider</strong> — too high early on can distort geometry</div>

    </div>

<section id="glossary">

  <div class="section-header fade-in">

    <span class="section-number">08</span>

    <h2 class="section-title">Glossary</h2>

  </div>


  <dl class="glossary-list fade-in">

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Batch Processing</dt><dd>Running multiple renders simultaneously rather than one at a time. Useful for generating all views of a project (exterior, interior, aerial) in a single queue. Available on Pro and higher plans.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Creativity Slider</dt><dd>A control in the Enhancer tool that determines how much the AI interprets vs. preserves your input image. Low settings stay close to your original geometry; high settings give the AI more freedom to reinterpret. Start in the mid-range for most architectural workflows.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Enhancement</dt><dd>The process of transforming an input image, such as a white model render or sketch, into a photorealistic output using a text prompt. Enhancement adds materials, lighting, and context. It is a separate step from upscaling.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Enhancer</dt><dd>Krea's primary tool for architectural workflows. It takes an uploaded image and a text prompt and generates a photorealistic version of the input. Preserves the underlying geometry while adding materials, lighting, and environment.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Image Generator</dt><dd>Krea's tool for generating images from scratch using only a text prompt, with no input image required. Useful for early concept visuals and mood boards when no model or sketch exists yet.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Image-to-Image</dt><dd>A workflow where an existing image is used as the starting point for a new generation. In Krea, this is the basis of the Enhancer tool: your white model or sketch is the input image, and the prompt guides the output.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Input Image</dt><dd>The image you upload to Krea as the basis for enhancement. In architectural workflows this is typically a white model render, sketch, or low-resolution render exported from Rhino, SketchUp, or Revit.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)</dt><dd>A method of fine-tuning the AI on a specific set of reference images. In architecture, LoRA training can be used to teach Krea a particular design style, material palette, or brand aesthetic so that all outputs are consistent across a project or practice.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Massing Model</dt><dd>An early-stage 3D model that represents the overall form and volume of a building without material or detail. Often used interchangeably with white model. A common starting point for Krea workflows.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Output Image</dt><dd>The photorealistic render generated by Krea after processing your input image and prompt. Can be further refined by iterating on the prompt or upscaled to 4K for presentation.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Photorealistic Render</dt><dd>A render that closely resembles a real photograph, with accurate materials, lighting, shadows, and environmental context. The primary goal of most Krea architectural workflows.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Prompt</dt><dd>The text instruction you write to describe what the finished image should look like. In Krea's Enhancer, the prompt works alongside your input image: the image defines the geometry, the prompt defines materials, lighting, context, and mood.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Realtime</dt><dd>Krea's live generation tool that produces instant visual output as you type or draw. Useful for rapid design exploration and iterating on facade options or material combinations in real time.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Style Transfer</dt><dd>Applying the visual style of one image or reference to another. In Krea, this can be achieved through prompting or LoRA training to generate outputs that match a specific aesthetic or precedent project.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>Upscaling</dt><dd>The process of increasing the resolution of an image while sharpening detail and recovering clarity. In Krea, upscaling is a separate step from enhancement: enhancement adds content, upscaling enlarges and refines it. The Basic plan supports up to 4K; the Max plan supports up to 22K.</dd></div>

    <div class="gloss-item"><dt>White Model</dt><dd>A 3D model rendered without any materials or textures, appearing entirely white or light grey. Used in architecture to communicate form and proportion. In Rhino, the Arctic display mode produces this look. The most common input image for Krea's Enhancer in architectural workflows.</dd></div>

  </dl>
